First things first:

Kids. Can. Change. The. World.

It's a fact, not a fantasy or wish or dream. They can and they are and we're going to meet a whole PILE of them who are making the world a more awesome place to thrive.

As we meet more and more amazing kids we'll update this page. So far we've interviewed 2, have 2 more on the docket, and hope to eventually meet amazing kids in EVERY STATE in the US (and DC)!

Here are a few to get you started!


Max  was 5 years-old when his mom's best friend died of breast cancer. That October he asked to have a lemonade stand to raise money for breast cancer research. TEN years later he's still doing it and has raised more than $100,000! Check out Max's site and support the cause!


When Anna was 9 years-old she started to notice the problem of homelessness  in Pasadena, CA. She researched and found out that many of those people were children and families. Within a few weeks she had put together a garage sale and raised more than $1,000 to help homeless families in her neighborhood! How do you spell "Awesome"? A-N-N-A.


You've seen him on TEDx, Dr. Os and the Colbert Report, and we're hoping to meet him on the road! Jack Andraka is the kid inventer-scientist-philathropist who, at the age of 15, invented a simple test that detects pancreatic cancer at it's earliest stages for about $.03 per test. DUDE. Jack rocks! Learn more at his site!


Kendall decided that not only did she need to help the homeless, but she also needed to help young women realize that they are capable of ANYTHING. So she learned to build a house. But not just any house. A house that could be used or sold to help homeless people in Monterey, CA. And she did it! Read her story and watch the TEDx talk on her site.


If you need a dose of AWESOME then go check out 9 year-old Robby (aka Kid President). This young man not only inspires others but he does it with love and a dance break. Enjoy a dose of Love with his pep talk, then follow-it-up with his true story.
